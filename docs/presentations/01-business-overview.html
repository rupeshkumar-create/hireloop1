Business & technology overview

Two AI agents. One shared candidate graph. A multi-market recruiting marketplace.

Product · Architecture · Vendors · July 2026

What it is

AI-run recruiting marketplace

Hireschema matches candidates and hiring managers with agents that do the work — not just chatbots on a job board.

Candidate side

Aarya

Career partner: profile, voice/text chat, scored job matches, one-tap intros.

DB
Shared graph
Recruiter side

Nitya

Pipeline partner: JD intake, candidate shortlists, warm intro outreach via Gmail.

Problem

Why this exists

Candidates

Noise, no leverage

Apply-spam, opaque ATS black holes, cold LinkedIn DMs that never land.

Recruiters

Sourcing is expensive

Boolean search, agency fees, stale inbound — hard to reach warm, verified talent fast.

Market

IN · US · UK (+)

Multi-region marketplace: market-scoped jobs, local currency, region-aware OTP.

Candidate value

What candidates get

  • LinkedIn signup → profile auto-built (LinkedIn + CV)
  • Aarya chat (text + voice) that learns preferences
  • Scored matches with skill gaps and LLM rationale
  • “Find jobs / Find new” pulls live roles via Apify
  • One-tap Request Intro — Aarya + Nitya handshake via DB
  • Cold email from their Gmail (OAuth) — deliverability + ToS-safe
North star

Less applying. More introductions.

The product wins when a candidate gets a real HM conversation without spraying applications.

Recruiter value

What recruiters get

Role in minutes

Create JD manually or paste a career-page URL — Firecrawl/HTML extract fills the form.

Public apply link

Publish → /r/{slug} — candidates land, sign up, and see the role in Jobs.

Graph-backed search

Match against the shared candidate graph (embeddings + skills + constraints), not a generic résumé dump.

Intro pipeline

Nitya drafts and sends warm intros (Gmail OAuth path). Status lives in intro_requests.
